01 · The Problem

Modern insulation wasn't designed for your home.

Victorian and Edwardian walls were designed to breathe — absorbing and releasing moisture as conditions changed. Sealing them with modern vapour barriers interrupts this cycle, trapping moisture inside the structure.

"We spent £14,000 on cavity fill insulation. Within eight months, the plaster was staining and we had mould behind the new kitchen units. The original surveyor never warned us." — Homeowner, Grade II listed Georgian terrace, Bath

Impermeable retrofits create hygrothermal failure in solid-wall masonry.

Pre-1919 construction relies on vapour diffusion equilibrium. A standard PIR or mineral wool system with foil facing creates a dew-point trap at the inner leaf — accelerating masonry decay and timber rot.

"Interstitial condensation behind an impermeable board system destroyed original lath and plaster within two winters. Historic England guidance Section 6.4 is unambiguous — and too often ignored." — Conservation Architect, practice submission to IHBC

Most installers fit the wrong product in the wrong building — without knowing it.

Standard training doesn't cover pre-1919 construction physics. Fitting modern board or cavity systems in solid-wall or listed buildings creates liability exposure — and a growing wave of callbacks you can't bill for.

"I'd been doing loft and cavity work for twelve years before I realised I'd probably caused damp problems in at least four Victorian properties. That's what made me retrain." — Retrofit Installer, West Midlands (BreathTherm certified)

Why conventional insulation fails older homes

Solid walls breathe: vapour barriers don't

Pre-1919 masonry relies on capillary action and vapour diffusion. Impermeable insulation blocks this cycle, forcing moisture to accumulate inside the wall structure rather than dispersing naturally.

The energy performance paradox

Wet walls conduct heat up to 3× faster than dry ones. Conventional insulation that causes dampness can actively worsen energy performance over time — the opposite of its intended purpose.

Listed building consent complications

Local planning authorities increasingly refuse conventional insulation materials on listed or conservation area properties — or require removal of previously fitted systems that have caused damage.

Loss of Heritage Character

Bulky systems alter window reveals, mouldings and proportions: the very features that define a Georgian or Victorian building. BreatheTherm achieves excellence in as little as 50mm.
