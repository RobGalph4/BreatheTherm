The Problem

Traditional homes need a different kind of insulation.

Most UK homes built before 1919 have solid walls that were designed to absorb and release moisture naturally. Modern insulation seals this process shut. The result is a "plastic bag effect" — moisture becomes trapped, condensation forms inside walls, and the building fabric slowly decays.

BreatheTherm is engineered specifically for buildings that breathe — allowing moisture to migrate safely through the wall while dramatically improving thermal performance.

Understanding the Risk

The "plastic bag" effect

Why conventional insulation fails heritage buildings

Solid masonry walls naturally absorb moisture from rain and internal condensation, then release it during warm, dry spells. This cyclic movement is the building's own weather management system — one that has protected structures for hundreds of years.

When you apply vapour-impermeable insulation, you interrupt this cycle. Moisture accumulates on both sides of the barrier. It has nowhere to go. Over months and years this leads to interstitial condensation, mould, frost damage, and accelerated decay of lime mortar and timber elements.

Many thousands of well-intentioned retrofits have caused significant damage. BreatheTherm is the alternative — a fully vapour-open mineral system that works with the building, not against it.

Why It Works

Built on the principles

BreatheTherm's system is designed around how traditional solid-wall buildings actually behave — not how modern building science assumes they do.

💧

Vapour-Open by Design

Every layer — from board to lime render — is carefully selected for high vapour permeability, creating an uninterrupted breathable assembly throughout the wall.

🧱

Mineral-Based Materials

No plastics, no foam, no synthetic barriers. Mineral wool boards and lime-based coatings that are chemically inert, fire-resistant, and durable for the life of the building.

Eliminates Cold Bridging

Continuous insulation application across the full building envelope addresses cold bridges at junctions, floors, and window reveals — the hidden source of significant heat loss.
